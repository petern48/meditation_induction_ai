Find a comfortable seated position with your back supported, either on a chair or on the floor. You can close your eyes or keep them lightly closed, focusing your attention inward. Take a few deep breaths and allow your body to relax, letting go of any tension or discomfort.

Bring your attention to your breath, feeling the sensation of the air moving in and out of your body. Notice the sensation of the breath in your nostrils, the rise and fall of your chest or belly, and the gentle movement of your breath. Allow your breath to become deeper and more relaxed with each passing moment.

Now, bring your attention to different parts of your body, starting from your toes and moving up to the top of your head. As you focus on each area, allow any tension or discomfort to release and melt away. Notice how your body feels as you breathe, and allow yourself to relax even deeper.

As you continue to breathe and focus on your body, imagine any stress or tension leaving your body through your feet or fingertips. Allow yourself to feel light and relaxed, like a heavy weight has been lifted off your shoulders.

Take a few more deep breaths and continue to focus on your body, allowing yourself to feel calm and centered. When you're ready, slowly open your eyes and take a few moments to notice how you feel before returning to your day.

Remember, school can be challenging, but you have the power to approach it with a calm and focused mind. Take a few deep breaths, and trust that you can handle whatever comes your way. this meditation is just a tool to help you manage stress and find calm in your busy school schedule.

Be patient and kind to yourself, and make time for meditation whenever you need it. Take care!
